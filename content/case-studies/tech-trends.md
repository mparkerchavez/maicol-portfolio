# Enterprise Tech Trends, AI Narrative and Adoption Enablement

I contributed AI-focused trend research and editorial synthesis for an annual Tech Trends publication distributed through internal platforms and amplified through a monthly internal intranet campaign.

The work supported senior business and technology leaders by providing a practical point of view on emerging capabilities, what was hype, what was durable, and how to think about readiness and responsible adoption.

## 01 /// THE CHALLENGE

### Staying ahead of AI acceleration.

As AI capabilities advanced rapidly, it became harder for organizations to maintain a shared, realistic understanding of what was emerging, what was hype, and what would matter over the next 3 to 5 years.

Without a clear narrative grounded in signals and evidence, teams risked becoming reactive. They chased new developments after they surfaced instead of building informed readiness over time.

The Tech Trends report helped senior business and technology leaders track where AI and other emerging technologies were heading.

Over time, AI touched almost every trend area. That made it important to frame AI evolution in a way that was actionable across business units, not just as a standalone topic.

## 02 /// THE STRATEGY

### Turning signals into an enterprise point of view.

We treated Tech Trends as an annual synthesis of signals, not a retrospective summary.

I authored AI-focused sections by combining external research and industry reporting with perspectives from external ecosystem partners, including startups and industry operators.

I then translated what we observed into an enterprise lens: what was emerging, what was becoming practical, and what teams should pay attention to next.

The annual report was organized around seven enterprise themes across Amplified Intelligence, Client Experience, FinTech, Workplace Technology, and other domains.

To make the publication actionable and consistent across contributors, each trend followed a standardized decision template with sections such as Trend Brief, Why it Matters, and Key Signals.

I also designed lightweight Copilot workflows to tag documents, surface cross-document signals, and provide writing feedback.

We treated automation as a directional accelerator, not a source of truth. Human editorial judgment remained the final gate.

I focused my primary contributions on Workplace Technology and my secondary contributions on Amplified Intelligence, applying an AI lens to how people work and how leaders evaluate applied capabilities over time.

## 03 /// THE OUTCOME

### Building shared context for smarter decisions.

The AI narrative work helped leaders and teams interpret rapid shifts in generative AI and emerging capabilities through an enterprise lens.

The work gave the organization a more deliberate way to discuss readiness, responsible use, and where to focus attention.

I delivered a State of AI briefing within a business unit, translating Tech Trends research into implications, sharing follow-up resources, and facilitating stakeholder Q&A tailored to near-term needs.

Beyond formal communications, I used the research and narrative assets in an embedded way during project work, translating external signals into practical implications for what to explore next.

## 04 /// OTHER STRATEGIC WORK

### Related initiatives.

AI vendor evaluation framework: Supported the investment group as AI use cases accelerated by evaluating vendor and platform options for orchestration and adjacent capabilities.

I created an evaluation scorecard and comparison matrix, co-authored the recommendation readout with the senior product manager, and presented findings to stakeholders.

Evaluation criteria included developer experience, integration fit, retrieval-augmented generation patterns, observability, agentic capabilities, and solution maturity.

I recommended using LangChain as an initial foundation for rapid prototyping because it was mature for the moment and had broad ecosystem support.

In discussions with lead engineers, we surfaced a key tradeoff: LangChain abstractions were acceptable for rapid prototyping but raised concerns for complex production workflows.

The team later chose to build a custom orchestration layer rather than continue with LangChain in production.

Copilot Studio architecture strategy: Partnered with a solutions engineer to represent architecture considerations and facilitate alignment with platform owners and information security.

The segmentation approach covered development through production stages, accounting for the iterative nature of generative AI workflows.

Data access constraints were a key factor in the decision, including production vs non-production and masked data considerations.

Copilot Studio prototype: Built a prototype to extract structured data points from research documents, enabling a second agent to analyze abstractions across sources and surface candidate trends.

The prototype supported the Tech Trends research process and helped the team evaluate Copilot Studio capabilities and limitations firsthand.
