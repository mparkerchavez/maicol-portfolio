# Scaling AI Discovery Through Innovation Sprints

I led 20+ innovation sprints and designed AI-focused workshops for client experience leaders and teams.

The work helped cross-functional partners move from broad AI interest to prioritized opportunities rooted in user needs, aligned to business outcomes, and shaped by enterprise implementation constraints.

## 01 /// THE CHALLENGE

### From AI curiosity to business-ready use cases.

As interest in AI accelerated, teams across the client experience space surfaced many ideas.

The hard part was turning that interest into business-ready opportunities rooted in user needs, measurable outcomes, and clear constraints.

Work was often framed as "can we use AI here?" rather than grounded in customer needs and implementation reality.

Without a shared structure for prioritization and alignment, it was difficult for leaders and teams to decide what to pursue, what to defer, and how to connect initiatives to business outcomes.

Data readiness, integration complexity, and change management varied across teams, which made the decision space harder to navigate.

## 02 /// THE STRATEGY

### A repeatable sprint framework for AI discovery and alignment.

I used innovation sprints as a repeatable way to move teams from AI ideas to decisions.

Across 20+ sprints, the approach paired human-centered problem framing with an enterprise lens, helping teams define where AI could add value and what would matter for adoption.

To scale that approach, I designed two AI-focused workshops.

The cross-functional AI discovery workshop brought together 30+ participants across Sales, Marketing, and Client Experience to build empathy for priority users, identify high-priority opportunities, and filter out ideas that would not get meaningful lift from AI.

The senior leader alignment workshop shifted the conversation from use cases to capabilities, enabling leaders to align on cross-pillar business capabilities AI could enable.

Each pillar evaluated opportunities across customer needs, fit with AI capabilities, and implementation considerations.

Data readiness was consistently the primary constraint, alongside integration complexity and change management.

Outputs included empathy maps, value maps, prioritized opportunity lists, a cross-pillar readout, and an executive capability lineage view.

## 03 /// THE OUTCOME

### Clearer priorities and better decision quality.

The sprint approach helped teams and leaders move faster from open questions to clearer decisions.

Instead of debating AI in the abstract, the workshops created shared context around user needs, priority opportunities, and feasibility constraints.

Across pillars, teams used the empathy and value mapping outputs to validate and refine existing initiatives, compare workshop priorities to current roadmaps, and reconsider sequencing where constraints made certain ideas less viable.

At the leadership level, the capability lineage and value chain mapping made alignment gaps visible before deeper investment.

Follow-up execution and refinement was owned by delivery leaders, supported by the alignment artifacts produced through the workshops.

## 04 /// RELATED WORK

### Broader facilitation portfolio.

Enterprise-wide innovation sprint program: Facilitated approximately two dozen innovation sprints across investment operations, client groups, and technology teams.

Each sprint moved cross-functional groups from ambiguous challenges to validated learnings and prioritized next steps.

Design thinking workshops for business outcomes: Created and facilitated custom design thinking workshops tailored to specific business challenges.

Workshops ranged from 30 to 50 person sessions translating customer needs into AI-shaped opportunities, to smaller team sessions defining opportunity areas and prioritized experiments.

Remote workshop innovation: During the COVID-19 pandemic, adapted and led the company's first remote design sprints using virtual collaboration tools.

The work maintained engagement and outcome quality while establishing virtual facilitation techniques that became repeatable.
